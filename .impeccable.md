## Design Context

### Users
Houston locals, multi-generational regulars, and visitors discovering the city's food scene. Primary jobs: browse the pie menu, confirm hours/location, and place holiday or special-occasion orders. Browsing happens on mobile during lunch or evenings. The site must feel immediately trustworthy — this is a family business people have relied on for decades.

### Brand Personality
Warm, nostalgic, playful. "Out of this world" is the actual tagline. A heritage institution (since 1967) with a retro space-age twist — the flying saucer is the brand mascot. Fun and approachable, never pretentious or clinical. Think: hand-painted diner sign crossed with a 1960s NASA brochure.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Light. Bakeries and food sites are browsed in daylight; the warm parchment palette is correct and should be preserved.
- **Tone**: Retro-warm. The space theme should feel more "1960s World's Fair" than sci-fi cold. Warm creams, deep navies, and the brand blue (#020169) as the primary accent.
- **References**: emporiumpies.com (warmth + craft), tartinebakery.com (food-first photography), igloo.inc (fun, character-led animation).
- **Anti-references**: Generic SaaS landing pages, dark-mode neon aesthetics, anything that feels like a tech product.
- **Locked brand elements**: #020169 (saucer SVG + hero headlines only), Fredoka (logo font), Pacifico (tagline font) — do not replace these.

### Design Principles
1. **Food is the hero** — pie photography should be the first thing that reads, not UI chrome.
2. **Warmth over coolness** — every design decision should make the site feel like a warm kitchen, not a spaceship interior.
3. **Heritage is a feature** — "since 1967" is a trust signal; lean into it visually with texture, craft, and specificity.
4. **The saucer earns its place** — the space theme is playful punctuation, not the whole story. Use it selectively and with wit.
5. **Accessible to everyone** — multi-generational audience means no small text, no low-contrast traps, no modals that frustrate older users.
